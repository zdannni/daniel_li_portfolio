Research as practice, not checklist.

The hard part of user research is rarely the methods. It is the judgment calls underneath: what to scope, which trade-offs to make, and how to land findings so teams actually act on them.

That belief shapes how I work, and it is what I hope comes through when you read the case studies.

More about my approach →

  1. Scope before you script

    Strong research starts by narrowing the messy question into something answerable. The framing decision matters more than the protocol.

  2. Methods chosen for reasons

    Every method carries trade-offs. I sequence interviews, surveys, diary studies, and sprints deliberately, and I can explain why each one is there.

  3. Synthesize for judgment, not volume

    Good synthesis surfaces the two or three findings that actually change decisions. It compresses rather than accumulates.

  4. Communicate so teams can act

    Research is only as valuable as the decisions it shapes. How a finding lands with stakeholders is part of the research, not an afterthought.
