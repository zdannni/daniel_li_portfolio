About me

Hi, I'm Daniel.

I got into user research sideways. I was working as a lab manager in a social psychology lab at UCSD when a PhD student asked me to help research competitors for a mental health startup she was building. That was my first taste of the field, and three things clicked at once: I love observing people, I love finding patterns, and I love seeing research change a real decision. UXR blended all three.

I went on to study Information at the University of Michigan, where I learned that methods are the part of research you can teach in a semester. The judgment calls are the part that takes years.

When I'm not at work: I watch films. I like the color green, which you may have noticed by now. I cook. And I collect kitchenwares. (I kept a carbon steel pan in sharp condition for two years, seasoned it daily. Unfortunately, I had to give it away when I moved out. Still in the mourning phase)
